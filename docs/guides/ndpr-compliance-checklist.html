Introduction

The Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) is Nigeria's principal data protection legislation, issued by the National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA) in January 2019. The NDPR aims to safeguard the rights of natural persons to data privacy and establishes a framework for ensuring that organizations process personal data in a fair, lawful, and transparent manner.

Compliance with the NDPR is not just a legal obligation but also a business imperative. Organizations that fail to comply with the NDPR may face significant penalties, including fines of up to 2% of annual gross revenue or ₦10 million, whichever is greater. Beyond the financial penalties, non-compliance can lead to reputational damage, loss of customer trust, and business disruption.

Who Must Comply with the NDPR?

The NDPR applies to:

  • All organizations that process the personal data of Nigerian residents
  • Organizations that process personal data in Nigeria, regardless of the nationality of the data subjects
  • Public and private sector organizations of all sizes
  • Organizations based outside Nigeria that process the personal data of Nigerian residents

Key NDPR Requirements

The NDPR establishes several key requirements that organizations must meet to ensure compliance. Understanding these requirements is the first step toward developing a comprehensive compliance program.

Lawful Processing

Organizations must process personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently. This means having a valid legal basis for processing, such as consent, contract, legal obligation, vital interests, public interest, or legitimate interests.

Consent

When relying on consent as the legal basis for processing, organizations must ensure that consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous. Consent must be obtained through a clear affirmative action and must be as easy to withdraw as it is to give.

Data Subject Rights

Organizations must respect and facilitate the rights of data subjects, including the right of access, right to rectification, right to erasure, right to restrict processing, right to data portability, and right to object to processing.

Data Protection Impact Assessment

Organizations must conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for processing activities that are likely to result in a high risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, particularly when using new technologies.

Data Protection Officer

Organizations that process large volumes of personal data must appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee data protection strategy and implementation. The DPO must have expert knowledge of data protection law and practices.

Breach Notification

Organizations must report data breaches to NITDA within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach and must notify affected data subjects without undue delay. Organizations must also maintain a record of all data breaches.

Privacy Policy

Organizations must maintain a clear and accessible privacy policy that informs data subjects about how their personal data is collected, processed, stored, and protected. The policy must be written in clear, plain language.

International Transfers

Organizations must ensure that personal data is not transferred to a country outside Nigeria unless that country ensures an adequate level of protection for the rights and freedoms of data subjects in relation to the processing of personal data.

Data Security

Organizations must implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including encryption, pseudonymization, and regular testing of security measures.

Record Keeping

Organizations must maintain records of processing activities, including the purposes of processing, categories of data subjects and personal data, recipients of personal data, and time limits for erasure.

NDPR Compliance Checklist

Use this comprehensive checklist to assess your organization's compliance with the NDPR and identify areas that need attention. The checklist is organized by key compliance areas, with specific action items for each area.

1Data Governance and Accountability

  • □Appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) or designate someone responsible for data protection
  • □Develop and implement a data protection policy
  • □Create and maintain records of processing activities
  • □Conduct regular data protection training for staff
  • □Implement a data protection by design and by default approach
  • □Establish a process for conducting Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs)

2Lawful Basis for Processing

  • □Identify and document the lawful basis for each processing activity
  • □Implement a consent management system for processing based on consent
  • □Ensure consent is freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous
  • □Maintain records of consent, including when and how it was obtained
  • □Implement a process for handling consent withdrawal

3Privacy Notices and Transparency

  • □Develop a clear and comprehensive privacy policy
  • □Ensure the privacy policy is easily accessible on your website
  • □Include all required information in the privacy policy (purposes of processing, categories of data, recipients, retention periods, data subject rights, etc.)
  • □Provide privacy notices at the point of data collection
  • □Use clear, plain language that is easy for data subjects to understand
  • □Regularly review and update privacy notices

4Data Subject Rights

  • □Implement a process for handling data subject access requests
  • □Establish procedures for rectifying inaccurate personal data
  • □Develop a process for erasing personal data when requested
  • □Implement mechanisms for restricting processing when requested
  • □Enable data portability for personal data provided by the data subject
  • □Establish a process for handling objections to processing
  • □Train staff on how to recognize and handle data subject requests

5Data Security

  • □Implement appropriate technical measures to protect personal data (encryption, access controls, etc.)
  • □Implement appropriate organizational measures (policies, procedures, training)
  • □Regularly test, assess, and evaluate the effectiveness of security measures
  • □Implement a process for regularly backing up personal data
  • □Establish procedures for restoring personal data in the event of a physical or technical incident

6Breach Notification

  • □Develop a data breach response plan
  • □Implement procedures for detecting, reporting, and investigating data breaches
  • □Establish a process for notifying NITDA within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach
  • □Develop templates for breach notifications to NITDA and affected data subjects
  • □Maintain a record of all data breaches, including the facts of the breach, its effects, and remedial actions taken
  • □Regularly test and update the breach response plan

7International Transfers

  • □Identify all transfers of personal data outside Nigeria
  • □Assess whether recipient countries provide adequate protection for personal data
  • □Implement appropriate safeguards for international transfers (standard contractual clauses, binding corporate rules, etc.)
  • □Obtain NITDA approval for international transfers where required
  • □Maintain records of all international transfers

8Data Protection Impact Assessment

  • □Develop criteria for determining when a DPIA is required
  • □Establish a process for conducting DPIAs
  • □Create templates and guidance for conducting DPIAs
  • □Ensure DPIAs are conducted before beginning new high-risk processing activities
  • □Maintain records of all DPIAs conducted
  • □Implement measures to address risks identified in DPIAs

Compliance is an Ongoing Process

Remember that compliance with the NDPR is not a one-time exercise but an ongoing process. Regularly review and update your data protection practices to ensure continued compliance, especially when introducing new processing activities or technologies. The NDPR Toolkit provides components and utilities to help you maintain compliance over time.

Compliance Roadmap

Achieving NDPR compliance can seem daunting, but breaking it down into manageable steps can make the process more approachable. Here's a suggested roadmap for your compliance journey:

1

Assessment

Begin by assessing your current data protection practices against the NDPR requirements. Use the compliance checklist provided above to identify gaps and areas for improvement.

2

Planning

Develop a compliance plan based on your assessment. Prioritize high-risk areas and quick wins. Assign responsibilities and set deadlines for implementation.

3

Implementation

Implement the necessary measures to address the gaps identified in your assessment. Use the NDPR Toolkit components to implement key compliance features such as consent management, data subject rights handling, and breach notification.

4

Documentation

Document your compliance efforts, including policies, procedures, and records of processing activities. This documentation is essential for demonstrating compliance to regulators and stakeholders.

5

Training

Train staff on data protection principles, the NDPR requirements, and your organization's specific policies and procedures. Ensure that everyone understands their role in maintaining compliance.

6

Monitoring and Review

Regularly monitor and review your compliance program to ensure it remains effective. Conduct periodic audits, address any new compliance gaps, and update your practices as needed.

Need Additional Help?

If you need additional help with NDPR compliance, consider consulting with a data protection professional or legal expert who specializes in Nigerian data protection law. While the NDPR Toolkit provides valuable tools and guidance, professional advice can help ensure that your compliance program addresses your organization's specific needs and risks.
